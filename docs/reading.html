Selection of books, articles, podcasts and guides I have read and shaped me into who I am.

Software Design
Aa a Rails developer I respect their doctrine of not falling into one paradigm. So I like reading about all kinds of styles. I don't agree with all of them but I like knowing and understanding them well.

  • Practical Object Oriented Design in Ruby: basics of OOP
  • 99 bottles of OOP: basics of SOLID
  • Effective Java: basics of design patterns
  • Sustainable Web Development with Ruby on Rails: opinionated way of organising Rails deviating from OO in favor of Procedural (Service Objects)
  • On writing software well: DHH's vision on how to apply and extend Rails
  • Code I like: how code at Basecamp looks (by Jorge)
  • Martin Fowler: general software design, architecture
  • Kent Beck: general software design, architecture
  • Gitlab guides: colleciton of very detailed and high quality guidelines
  • Gitlab repo: a repo you should have locally to refer to
  • Thoughtbot guides: a good collection of guidelines
  • Evil martians: many interesting articles
  • Code with Jason podcast: many interesting interviews with prominent Rails developers

Leadership, management, organisation
Leadership is best thought by doing. But reading helps you find the right ideas at the right time.

  • The Staff Engineer's Path: basic introduction to the IC leadership track
  • Rand's Slack: goldmine on any tech leadership topic
  • BDD workshop by Gáspár Nagy: workshop that thought me about discovery, example mapping and BDD
  • Leadership workshops by TPC: basic introduction workshops to leadership
  • Startup CTO Handbook: survival guide on being a CTO
  • Shape Up: an alternative to SCRUM focussed on fixed-time variable-scope projects
  • Alex Komoroske: a Product Manager with very flavored ideas
  • The Rework Podcast: insight into how Basecamp leadership works
  • Thierry de Pauw: a colleague with interesting ideas
  • Dave Farley: ideas on tech leadership
  • The Phoenix Project: a classic IT tale
  • The five dysfunctions of a team: a classic leadership book

Academic books
Many of these books are probably a bit outdated but still offer some insight into basic Computer Science principles.

  • Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning: theoretical basics of Machine Learning
  • Engineering Software as a Service: basics on SaaS
  • Introduction to Algorithms: classic Algorithms explained
  • Principes van databases: theoretical basics of database (design)
  • Building Scalable Web Sites
